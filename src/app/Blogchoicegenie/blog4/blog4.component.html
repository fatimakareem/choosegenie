4 Clever Hacks: Save Big On Energy Usage

Introduction

The thick heat of summer is the beginning of a series of very heavy electricity bills.

Hadn’t you have to kick on the air conditioning a few times already? Thought so!

Did you know, a central air conditioning unit uses about of 3k watts of energy, whereas the average ceiling fan uses 60 watts! – that too when running on high.

If you run down your ceiling fan rather than AC, you will save a lot of money over the course of time.

ChoiceGenies has compiled some tactics to try in order to decrease your energy usage and increase savings, high time!

Save Money Hack # 1: Adjust Ceiling Fan to Counter-Clockwise in Summers

Adjust the direction of the ceiling fan in counter-clockwise.

Make sure the air blows down in summer.

According to the appropriateness of the seasons, most fans can be set for clockwise and counterclockwise.

If you are confused as to which direction to use and when to use, there is an easy way to get out of this uncertainty!

Standing beneath a fan, if you feel breeze immediately, know that it is set on the counter-clockwise.

By the way, counterclockwise is set on most models.

Save Money Hack # 2: Adjust Ceiling Fan to Clockwise in Winters

To circulate warm air through the room, run the fan – on low – in the clockwise direction in the winters.

To set it on clockwise, flip the switch or push the button near the base of the fan.

When blades run in this direction, the air will be pulled up in the center of the room and pushed down near the edges.

The mechanism forces warm air and cool air to mix in the room, keeping the room at a steadier temperature.

Save Money Hack # 3: Set Temperature Higher in Summers

Raise the temperature on your thermostat only by a few degrees.Do not let the ceiling fan bear all the burden.

The task of a ceiling fan is to circulate the cool air better, not to directly cool it per se.

It is the breeze effect making the room felt cooler than it actually is –

Thanks to the Breeze Effect!

Save Money Hack # 4: Not at home? Turn Off the Ceiling Fan

Bring in a programmable thermostat!

Set the air conditioning unit to turn off and on based on when you are home.

Trust us when we say, it will lead to big savings!

Unfortunately though, ceiling fans – most of them – do not offer this, be sure to turn it off before leaving home.

Final Words

You might discard it as an unnecessary now, room lighting matters!

Install the fan in a room with perfect lighting.

When in doubt, get the light assembly now.

It will save you consternation and money over the long haul.
